Oslob is a beautiful coastal town in Cebu, famed for its whale shark watching and stunning natural landscapes. Nestled along the southeastern coast, it offers breathtaking views of the ocean and lush surroundings. With its rich culture, warm hospitality, and delicious local cuisine, Oslob is a treasure trove of experiences.

Visitors can explore historical sites like The Cuartel, enjoy vibrant local festivals, and indulge in fresh seafood at local eateries. Oslob promises unforgettable adventures, making it a must-visit destination for every traveler.

Walk Through History

Origins and Meaning

Located in southeastern Cebu, Oslob was founded in 1848, with roots dating back to 1690 as a satellite community of Boljoon. Its name likely comes from the Visayan words for "to soak" or "to dip," reflecting a local tradition of soaking boiled bananas in salt.

The Cuartel

In the late 19th century, Oslob gained significance with the construction of The Cuartel, a coral stone barracks meant for Spanish soldiers. However, it was left unfinished due to the shift to American rule. This colonial legacy, combined with vibrant local traditions, highlights the rich cultural tapestry of Oslob today.

Nuestra Señora de la Inmaculada Concepción Parish Church

Established in 1830, is a significant landmark that showcases the town's Spanish colonial heritage. Its architectural style and role as a community center reflect Oslob's rich cultural history.

Where is Oslob?

The Municipality of Oslob is bordered to the north by Boljoon, to the west by Ginatilan and Samboan, to the east by the Cebu Strait, and to the south by Santander.

Oslob is conveniently located approximately 116 kilometers (72 miles) south of Cebu City. The drive typically takes about 3 to 4 hours by car, depending on traffic conditions. Public transportation is also readily available, with buses running regularly from Cebu City to Oslob; this bus journey usually takes around 4 to 5 hours and departs from the South Bus Terminal.

The nearest airport is Mactan-Cebu International Airport, located about 120 kilometers (75 miles) away. From the airport, travelers can take a taxi or shuttle to the bus terminal and then catch a bus to Oslob. Additionally, Oslob’s proximity to other popular tourist destinations, such as Moalboal (around 30 minutes away) and Dumaguete (approximately an hour by ferry), makes it an ideal base for exploring the region.

Faces of Oslob’s Governance

Oslob functions with a mayor-council government structure. The municipal leadership is led by the Mayor, serving as the chief executive, while the Vice Mayor oversees the Sangguniang Bayan
(Municipal Council). This council consists of elected councilors responsible for enacting local laws and regulations.

Jose C. Tumulak Jr.

Mayor of the Municipality of Oslob

Jose C. Tumulak Jr. is the Mayor of Oslob. He focuses on community development and sustainable tourism, promoting the town's whale shark watching and beautiful beaches. He aims to improve public services and the quality of life for residents.

Roger O. Trapa

VMayor of the Municipality of Oslob

Roger O. Trapa serves as the Vice Mayor of Oslob. He assists the Mayor in local governance and leads the Sangguniang Bayan (Municipal Council). Trapa is responsible for supporting the enactment of local laws and regulations, focusing on community needs and development.
